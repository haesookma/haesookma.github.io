Data Analysis for Statistics of Visitors to Major Tourist Attractions in South Korea

(Independent Study)

By: Haesook Ma
Sep 14, 2021

Introduction

(It’s a draft version; it will be updated soon.)

Since the first confirmed COVID-19 case on January 20, 2020, in Korea, there has been huge impact on tourism industry. Park and Park (2020) argue that ‘tourism-related sectors’ are even more vulnerable to the impact of COVID-19 because of the ‘lower resilience’ nature of the industry.′ The pandemic has led to closing the border of each country’, consequently the tourism industry lost its customers from overseas. Domestic traveling was also restricted to comply with the social distancing regulations.

However, since the lockdown lasts longer than a year, people look for alternative travel solutions. Bank of Korea (2021) points out that ‘voluntary participation in disease control efforts has weakened due to the public’s pandemic fatigue’. While complying with the quarantine and social distancing rules, people seek safer ways to travel. Sung et al. (2021) illustrates the novel trend in tourism. First, travelers look for outdoor tourist destinations because they are required to keep a social distancing and limit their movement to a minimum. Second, the pandemic ironically invigorates the domestic tourism because the borders are closed. In the concrete, travelers are looking for ‘untact’ tourist destinations such as hiking trails, forests, and parks. ‘Untact is a newly coined term in Korea with the negative prefix ‘un’ added to the word ‘contact’, referring to a behavioral disposition to minimize direct contact between people (Kim, 2018).′

The industrial trend changes can be detected by the analysis of up-do-date data. MY study examines new tourism trend after the COVID-19 by looking into data and ultimately establishes a short-term marketing strategy. (more marketing aspect will be added) Real-time market analysis has diverse benefits (De Luca et al. 2021). A traditional marketing strategy needs extensive market analysis and conducting surveys, so it takes time to establish a well-developed marketing strategy. On the other hand, a short-term marketing strategy can be developed timely and effectively.
