How to effectively use data to achieve a marketing plan

in the situation of COVID-19 using Statistics of Visitors to Major Tourist Attractions

By: Haesook Ma
Dec 1, 2021

Introduction

After the first confirmed COVID-19 case in South Korea on January 20, 2020, the external business environment has changed. Borders were closed, and social distancing measures were implemented to prevent the spread of the disease. Almost all businesses were affected, but tourism industry was the hardest hit. It is because the unique feature of the tourism industry, where human contact is inevitable (Park and Park, 2020). Thus, the pandemic has made the tourism market unpredictable.

In this fast changing environment, data can help us achieve the marketing plans. This study describes how data can be used to effectively achieve marketing plans in the context of unprecedented changes in the external business environment. First, we can understand the tourism market changes by observing the up-to-date data. Second, the data can be used to evaluate the marketing efforts. Lastly, tourism market forecasting can be done with data.

Conclusion

Data Analysis - Understanding the tourism market changes

The biggest change in the tourism market since the COVID-19 crisis is the decrease in tourists. The data showed that both outdoor and indoor tourists have decreased. Also, before the COVID-19, there was a certain trend in the number of tourists, but that has changed. Prior to the pandemic, the tourist spots under social sub_category was the most popular, but nature became the first.

One of the interesting findings is that the number of tourists under social sub-category began to increase from January 2021. It could be explained by the people’s desire to connect with others. Due to social distancing, people have been encouraged to stay home and keep having a distance from each other. Since government regulations lasted longer than expected, people began to travel again with safety precautions.

Performance Indicator - Evaluating the marketing efforts by data

Data can be used as a performance indicator, and the untact tourism market spots are used as an example in this study. Korea Tourism Organization conducted a marketing campaign to select and promote 100 untact tourism spots in 2020. According to the data, these spots attracted more tourists than other spots. Therefore, the growth rate of tourists in untact tourist spots could be used as one of the indicators to evaluate the performance.

Future Prediction - Forecasting the tourism market

The tourism market trend has changed since the first COVID-19 confirmed case in January 2020. Because of the government’s travel restrictions and social distancing measures, it is true that the tourism demand itself has decreased. However, since the pandemic has lasted longer, people are eager to travel although their preferences have somewhat changed. As previous data analysis showed, people tend to prefer outdoor activities in pursuit of social gathering. The new trend would continue for some time until the safety concerns are eliminated, so it can be incorporated into the upcoming marketing plans.
